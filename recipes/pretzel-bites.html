Pretzel Bites

Savory Perfection: Irrestible Pretzel Bites Recipe

Indulge in the delectable world of homemade pretzel bites with this easy-to-follow recipe. These savory delights boast a golden-brown exterior with a soft, doughy center, making them the ideal snack for any occasion. Elevate your culinary skills as you twist, boil, and bake your way to a batch of irresistibly delicious pretzel bites. Perfect for dipping into your favorite sauces or enjoying on their own, these little bites are sure to be a crowd-pleaser. Get ready to savor the warm, comforting aroma and taste the goodness of freshly baked pretzels in every bite!

Ingredients

  • 1½ cups water
  • 6 tablespoons butter, melted
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 (.25 ounce) package active dry yeast
  • 2½ teaspoons kosher salt
  • 4 cups all-purpose flour, or more as needed
  • 1 teaspoon vegetable oil
  • 3 quarts cold water
  • ½ cup baking soda
  • cooking spray
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tablespoon water

Steps

  1. Place 1½ cups water, butter, brown sugar, yeast, and salt in the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with the hook attachment. Mix until well combined. Let sit for about 5 minutes.
  2. Turn mixer on low speed and add 4 cups flour, 1 cup at a time. Add additional ½ cup flour if dough appears sticky. Increase speed to medium and knead until dough is smooth and pulls away from the sides of the bowl, 4 to 5 minutes. Sprinkle in more flour if dough still seems a little tacky.
  3. Oil a large mixing bowl. Add the ball of dough. Cover with plastic wrap and let rise in a warm place for about 1 hour.
  4. Preheat the oven to 425 degrees F (220 degrees C). Grease a sheet pan with cooking spray.
  5. Bring 3 quarts water to a boil in a large saucepan. Slowly add baking soda and return to a boil.
  6. Meanwhile, remove dough from the bowl, flatten into a disc shape, and cut into 8 wedges. Roll each wedge into a 24-inch rope. Cut each rope into 1-inch pieces.
  7. Drop batches of dough pieces into the boiling water for about 35 seconds. Remove to the prepared sheet pan. Beat egg with 1 tablespoon water; brush over the tops of each boiled dough piece. Repeat with remaining dough.
  8. Bake in the preheated oven until golden brown, 14 to 16 minutes. Cool slightly before saving.
